Particulate Matter (PM2.5)

Detects fine dust and smoke particles from the kitchen or nearby traffic.
  • Good: 0–12 µg/m³ (little to no health risk)
  • Moderate: 12.1–35 µg/m³ (sensitive individuals may experience minor irritation)
  • Hazardous: >35 µg/m³ (serious respiratory and heart issues)

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

Critical for measuring ventilation in the canteen. High levels typically indicate overcrowding or lack of fresh air.

  • Moderate: 801–1,000 ppm (slight drowsiness possible)
  • Hazardous: >1,000 ppm (headaches, sleepiness, reduced focus)

Volatile Organic Compounds (VOC)

Broad indicator for harmful gases, smoke, and chemical vapors in the air.
  • Good: 0–50 ppm (clean air, no harmful vapors)
  • Moderate: 51–100 ppm (presence of odors or light smoke)
  • Hazardous: >100 ppm (chemical vapors, ammonia, or smoke causing immediate irritation)
Good Moderate Poor
Current: 0.38 mg/m³

Tip: Open windows for ventilation and remove VOC sources like cooking fumes.
